## How The Pieces Connect

### 1. **Character Research â†’ Data Structure**
Feed Loop investigations documented 8 cases of professionals facing ethical dilemmas under structural constraint. This became `character-profiles.js` with:
- Quantified ethical scores
- Personality/response profiles (11 dimensions)
- What they did vs. what happened
- Why they were constrained

### 2. **Data â†’ Visual Reference**
Character data rendered as **1960s farm equipment specification sheets** in 4 authentic formats:
- **Test reports** for official documentation
- **Almanac pages** for cultivation context
- **Parts manuals** for assembly understanding
- **Pocket booklets** for quick reference

**Design rationale:** Agricultural technology metaphor, worker-facing documentation, print heritage, no personality cult (abstract botanical codes replace photos).

### 3. **Dilemmas â†’ Playable Scenarios**
Each character's constraint became a **negotiation scenario** where:
- User plays as the protagonist (ALLY)
- AI plays as the structural barrier (KEEPER)
- Negotiation reveals why individual ethics fails
- Educational goal: understand systems, not blame individuals

## Educational Arc

### Phase 1: **Read the Character Cards**
**Format:** Parts catalog reference sheets  
**Learning:** Who faced what dilemma, what they did, what constraints bound them

**Example:** Maya Chen card shows:
- Ethical awareness: 95%
- Ethical action: 45%
- Career risk taken: 30%
- Outcome: "Marginalized within company; research archived"

**Question provoked:** Why such a gap between awareness and action?

### Phase 2: **Negotiate as the Character**
**Format:** Centaur Box scenario  
**Learning:** Experience the structural forces that constrained them

**Example:** Play as Maya Chen negotiating with VP Growth:
- Try to reframe addiction as business risk
- Propose compromise (user controls, warnings)
- Discover VP has different optimization function
- Authority gap makes most moves unavailable
- Even "good" outcomes are marginal improvements

**Question provoked:** What would it take to change the system, not just negotiate within it?

### Phase 3: **Structural Analysis**
**Format:** Post-negotiation comparison  
**Learning:** Connect individual experience to systemic pattern

**Example:** After Maya scenario, show:
- Your outcome vs. what actually happened
- Why authority gap was so binding
- How metric incentives shaped VP's position
- What collective action would require

**Question provoked:** If 8 different professionals in 8 different scenarios all faced similar blocks, what does that say about the system?

## Design Philosophy

### Why 1960s Farm Equipment Aesthetic?

**Conceptual Fit:**
- Feed Loop = agricultural technology metaphor
- Practitioners = cultivars with growth characteristics
- Constraints = soil conditions, climate, seasonal limits
- Technical specs = ethical performance under constraint

**Visual Heritage:**
- Nebraska Tractor Test Laboratory format (official documentation)
- Old Farmer's Almanac tradition (seasonal wisdom, utility)
- Parts catalog vernacular (worker-facing, not marketing)
- Print optimization (accessible, durable, archival)

**Ideological Stance:**
- No glamour shots (abstract botanical codes, not personality cult)
- Worker documentation (parts counter reference, not consumer ad)
- Authentic typography (period-accurate fonts from actual catalogs)
- Humble elegance (craft of utility, not designed-to-impress)

### Why Centaur Box for Scenarios?

**Negotiation as Systems Analysis:**
- Playing as constrained actor reveals constraint
- AI opponent represents structural force (not person)
- "Winning" is ambiguous (marginal gains vs. systemic change)
- Failure teaches as much as success

**Educational Value:**
- Visceral understanding beats abstract lecture
- Users discover limits through trying
- Different approaches reveal negotiable vs. structural
- Collective patterns emerge across scenarios

**Replayability:**
- Different strategies yield different outcomes
- Users test "what if I had said X?"
- Compare approaches with others
- Learn what moves work/don't work

## The Core Insight

**Individual ethics consistently fails under institutional constraint.**

This isn't because people are badâ€”it's because:
- Authority is centralized (those with awareness lack power)
- Metrics are misaligned (optimize for engagement, not wellbeing)
- Precarity is weaponized (visa status, contract terms)
- Deadlines override rigor (investor demos, board pressure)
- Legal compliance becomes ethical ceiling ("it's compliant" = "it's fine")

**Solution space:**
- Collective action (individual negotiation has limited reach)
- Structural redesign (change incentives, not just decisions)
- Power redistribution (give authority to those with awareness)
- Alternative metrics (measure harm alongside engagement)

**This system documents, visualizes, and makes playable that insight.**

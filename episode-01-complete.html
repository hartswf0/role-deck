TECHNICAL BRIEF
NUTRIENT LOCK
GrowthPath — Corporate Learning Platform

The System

GrowthPath is Agronica's corporate learning management system (LMS) disguised as a garden simulation. Employees manage virtual "skill gardens" where courses appear as seeds, progress as growth stages, and completion as harvest. The gamification layer includes NPCs (like "Boundary Bob"), achievement badges, leaderboards, and persistent notifications.

The Technical Architecture

Behind the pastoral UI:

  • Engagement loops: Variable ratio reinforcement schedules (same psychology as slot machines)
  • Notification system: Personalized push timing optimized for maximum open rates
  • Social comparison: Leaderboards visible to managers, creating competitive pressure
  • Streak mechanics: "Garden health" decays without daily interaction
  • Analytics pipeline: Session duration, return frequency, anxiety sentiment analysis from in-app surveys

The Ethical Dilemma

Maya Chen, Learning & Development lead, discovers what the engagement team already knows: users report compulsive behavior, guilt when not engaging, and anxiety about falling behind. The data shows classic addiction markers.

She wants to present findings at the company's internal Harvest Summit. Her research shows the difference between pedagogically effective engagement (learners motivated by growth) vs. addictive dependency (learners trapped by fear of loss).

The Structural Constraint

Product and Growth teams reframe her research:

  • "Dependency" becomes "sticky engagement"
  • "Anxiety" becomes "passionate user base"
  • "Addictive patterns" become "habit formation success"

The same data. Different language. One gets celebrated; one gets silenced.

Why This Matters

Pedagogy suppression happens when business metrics override learning science. Maya has domain expertise in adult education, but product velocity wins. Her choice: speak truth and risk career consequences, or use sanitized language that preserves the harmful system.

Boundary Bob—the tutorial NPC designed to teach limits and self-paced learning—gets deprecated in the next version. Teaching boundaries is "growth-negative."

The Real-World Parallel

This scenario is based on documented cases of UX researchers and learning designers whose findings on compulsive app usage were reframed or suppressed by growth teams. See: Chivukula et al. (CHI '21), "A Fork in the Road" research on educator identity under constraint.

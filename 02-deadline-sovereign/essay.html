In the future, all harm will be v2

Announcing our commitment to iterative ethics
Office of Product Velocity
WEBERIAN IDEAL TYPE The "Deadline Sovereign" — Existential time pressure converts ethical salience into deferrable technical debt. The Sense of Responsibility→Activism trajectory is neutered by clock-time sovereignty. Harm becomes a backlog item.

FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE

We're proud to announce the General Availability of our product. After months of intensive development, we're bringing our vision to millions of users worldwide. This is just the beginning.

B1 — Bias is a backlog item

Transparency Note

During internal testing, our team identified several edge cases where the algorithm produced unexpected outputs for certain demographic groups. We've documented these patterns thoroughly and added them to our post-launch roadmap as P2 priority items.

We believe in shipping responsibly—which means shipping, then refining. Perfection is the enemy of progress.

"Risk displacement to 'post-launch monitoring'… converts ethical salience into deferrable technical debt." — Adapted from Weberian analysis

B2 — User pain is learning

We've built robust feedback mechanisms. Every incident report becomes a data point. Every complaint becomes a training signal. Our users aren't just consumers—they're co-creators of a better product. Their experiences in the wild provide insights no lab environment could replicate.

The Value Exchange

Yes, some early adopters may encounter friction. But they're also pioneers. They're participating in the future. That's the bargain of innovation: you get access to transformative technology; we get real-world feedback to make it better.

B3 — Investors are diverse, so the model is too

Questions have been raised about demographic representation in our training data. We want to assure stakeholders that our investor base includes partners from four continents, representing a wide range of perspectives and backgrounds. This diversity flows through to our priorities.

Representation matters—at the cap table and in the code.

B4 — Our ethics pipeline is CI/CD-adjacent

Technical Infrastructure

We've integrated ethics checkpoints into our deployment workflow. Each release candidate is evaluated against a growing set of heuristics. When potential issues are flagged, the system generates a ticket, assigns it to the relevant team, and tracks it through resolution.

This is ethics at scale: automated, measurable, continuous.

Research Context: Chivukula et al. describe practitioners who identify as "Learners"—those who desire to build ethics-focused knowledge before acting. But when deadlines override learning time, the Learner identity becomes performative. You can't learn what you don't have time to study. The result: "epistemic debt" masked as "agile iteration."

B5 — KPIs: incidents closed per tweet

We measure what matters. Our Trust & Safety dashboard tracks:

• Mean Time to Acknowledge (MTTA): Average hours between user report and team acknowledgment
• Incidents Resolved Per Sprint: Closed tickets tagged "bias," "fairness," or "harm"
• Social Sentiment Recovery Time: Hours from negative viral post to neutral baseline
• Regulatory Inquiry Response Rate: Percentage of agency questions answered within SLA

Last quarter, we closed 47% more incidents than we opened. Progress is measurable.

B6 — We are learners (after launch)

Our team embodies the "Learner" identity. We're committed to continuous education—reading papers, attending conferences, consulting experts. The difference is timing. We learn fastest when we have real data. Real data requires real users. Real users require launch.

So we launch, then we learn. It's the only honest sequence.

"Practitioners negotiate and mediate the ethical design complexity of everyday practice, encompassing organizational structures, applied ethics and knowledge of ethical practices, and personal values, commitments, and practices." — Gray, Chivukula et al., on ethical complexity

The complexity is real. The timeline is real. We navigate both.

B7 — Translators translate remorse

Incident Response Protocol

When harm occurs, our communications team—our "Translators"—converts technical post-mortems into public statements. They translate: "edge case in demographic weighting" becomes "isolated incident affecting small user subset." This isn't spin. It's clarity. Precision would confuse; translation serves.

Identity Claim: The "Translator" in CHI '21 refers to practitioners who "translate their knowledge into decisions or organizational change." But under deadline sovereignty, the Translator doesn't translate knowledge into better systems—they translate aftermath into acceptable narrative. The direction reverses.

B8 — Educators teach 'acceptable loss'

We've implemented mandatory training: "Responsible AI Decision-Making Under Constraint." The curriculum covers:

• Risk assessment frameworks
• Acceptable loss thresholds by market segment
• Triage protocols for conflicting harms
• Legal safe harbor principles
• Effective apology templates

Our people aren't just builders—they're educators. They teach each other how to build responsibly within reality.

B9 — Custodians maintain the memorial wall

We've created an internal memorial: a Slack channel called #lessons-learned where we document every incident, every edge case, every user harmed. It's searchable. It's permanent. New hires read it during onboarding.

We don't forget. We curate remembrance.

The Custodian Role

Someone maintains this archive. Someone adds the timestamps, the ticket links, the resolution notes. That's the "Custodian" identity—caring for the record even when the harm is closed, the user moved on, the feature deprecated.

B10 — Tomorrow is ethical

Here's what we've learned: ethics is a direction, not a destination. Every version is more ethical than the last. v1 had problems. v2 will have fewer. v3 fewer still. Asymptotically, we approach good.

But we had to start. We had to launch. Waiting for perfect meant never starting.

So we started imperfect. We'll end better.

Weberian Aside — What the Bit Comically Revealed

Innovation Reversed: The Activist trajectory—where Sense of Responsibility inscribes itself into design—collapses under time pressure. SoR exists, but can't act. It becomes feeling without function.

Pathologies Amplified: "Deadline Sovereign" converts moral deliberation into sprint planning. Harm becomes a ticket. Users become QA. Tomorrow becomes the answer to every "when."

Futures Obsoleted: A development culture with structural slack—time to learn unknowns before deciding. Instead: "ship under veil of ignorance," call it agility, measure the wreckage.

We're builders. We're learners. We're responsible.

We're just responsible iteratively.

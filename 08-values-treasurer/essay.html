Press Release · File 08

In the future, all principles will be tiered

Announcing our commitment to context-aware values
Office of Values & Strategy
WEBERIAN IDEAL TYPE The "Dual Steward" — Founder toggles between Custodian-of-Values and Optimizer-of-Livelihoods under fiduciary pressure. Concentrated client power forces geo-fencing of principles. Identity claims collide across organizational layers.

FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE

We've always been clear about our values: inclusion, transparency, user agency. These aren't slogans—they're commitments that guide every decision we make. Today, we're announcing an evolution in how we operationalize those values across our global footprint.

B1 — We stand proudly for inclusion, regionally

Inclusion means different things in different contexts. In San Francisco, it means Pride flags and pronouns in bios. In Dubai, it means respecting local norms while quietly supporting diverse teams. Both are inclusion—just contextualized.

We're introducing Regional Values Frameworks: tailored expressions of our core principles that honor cultural contexts. Same heart, different presentation.

B2 — Geo-ethics: values, but targeted

Our largest client—representing 40% of annual revenue—operates in markets with different regulatory and cultural environments. They've asked us to implement geo-targeted content moderation. Certain user profiles, visible in North America and Europe, would be suppressed in other regions.

After extensive deliberation, we've agreed. Here's why: universal enforcement of our values would mean losing the client, which would mean layoffs, which would mean less capacity to advocate for those values long-term. Strategic compromise preserves future leverage.

"Concentrated client power… value signaling vs. payroll obligations… geo-fencing as moral hedge." — Structural analysis

B3 — Optimizer: protect jobs by unpublishing people

The Optimizer role requires us to maximize value across constraints. The constraint: keep the client, keep the team employed. The value: do the least harm possible within that constraint.

We could refuse the client's request on principle. Then they leave. Then we lay off 30% of staff. Those people lose health insurance, salaries, stability. That's harm too. We're choosing the harm we can contain: regional suppression, not wholesale unemployment.

B4 — Translator: 'Alignment' means aligned cashflows

In our all-hands, we framed this as "market alignment." Different markets have different readiness levels for certain content. We're aligning our platform to meet users where they are. That's not censorship—it's localization.

The Translator role is linguistic: finding language that's technically true and strategically palatable. "Geo-targeted content policies" sounds better than "we hide queer users in homophobic markets." Both describe the same system. One preserves morale.

Research Context: Chivukula et al. describe practitioners who toggle between identity claims based on role and context. The founder as "Dual Steward" embodies this maximally: Custodian of mission in the morning, Optimizer of survival in the afternoon. The toggle isn't hypocrisy—it's structural necessity when two legitimate responsibilities conflict.

B5 — Educator: teach market forces empathy

We're launching internal education: "Economics of Ethics." The curriculum covers:

• Revenue concentration risk
• Fiduciary duties to employees
• Client negotiation constraints
• Long-term vs. short-term moral calculus
• Case studies in strategic compromise

The goal: help team members understand that ethical leadership isn't about purity—it's about trade-offs under constraint. We're educators helping our people think institutionally, not just morally.

B6 — Custodian: steward both soul and payroll

As founders, we're custodians of two things: the company's mission and the livelihoods it supports. When those conflict, we don't get to pick one. We have to steward the tension.

That means:

• Accepting the geo-targeting requirement
• Documenting our discomfort internally
• Setting a timeline to revisit (18 months)
• Using revenue to diversify client base
• Building leverage to renegotiate later

Stewardship isn't choosing between soul and payroll. It's protecting both, imperfectly, over time.

B7 — Harmonizer: announce a task force

We're forming a Values Alignment Task Force: cross-functional team examining how our principles translate across geographies. They'll deliver recommendations in Q3. This gives everyone a constructive channel for concern.

The Harmonizer role is procedural: creating forums that absorb dissent without blocking decisions. The task force won't reverse the client deal—it's already signed. But it gives people agency over the next version of this dilemma.

B8 — Ponderer: meditate on unit economics

Some decisions require sitting with discomfort. We're uncomfortable with geo-targeting. We're also uncomfortable with layoffs. There's no path that feels clean. So we ponder: we acknowledge the tension, we journal about it, we discuss it in therapy.

Deliberative practice—being a "Ponderer"—doesn't change the outcome. But it ensures we're making aware compromises, not unconscious ones. That matters, even if only to us.

"Identity claims can't inscribe into outcomes without structural levers… the A↔B↔C mediation fantasy." — Weberian analysis

B9 — Challenger: go viral

We anticipate that some will leak this decision. That's their right. If you believe we're morally compromised, exit with noise. Write a blog post. Go on Twitter. Create accountability through attention.

We respect Challengers. We also know: going viral may feel righteous, but it accelerates the exact outcome you fear. Negative press → client panic → deal collapse → layoffs. Your viral moment costs your former colleagues their jobs. Is that the trade you want?

B10 — Learner: diversify revenue (tomorrow)

Our long-term learning: concentrated revenue is concentrated risk, which is concentrated moral risk. One client shouldn't have 40% leverage. That's on us.

We're committing: within 24 months, no client will exceed 15% of revenue. That gives us the economic foundation to say "no" to future demands like this. We're learning. We're just learning expensively, and late.

Weberian Aside — What the Bit Comically Revealed

Innovation Reversed: The "Custodian-of-Values" identity should protect mission from market forces. Instead, market forces dictate which values get custodied where. Geography becomes moral jurisdiction.

Pathologies Amplified: Dual stewardship (values + livelihoods) collapses into sequential betrayal: betray values to protect jobs, betray trust to maintain values claim. The A↔B↔C mediation doesn't resolve tension—it distributes guilt.

Futures Obsoleted: Organizational models with structural buffers against client capture. Co-ops, public benefit corporations, mission-locked governance. Contexts where "we can't afford our values" isn't a viable sentence.

We believe in our values. We believe in our team.

We're just learning that believing isn't the same as affording.

In the future, all due diligence will be post hoc

On learning by launching and the MVP of knowledge
Office of Velocity & Learning
WEBERIAN IDEAL TYPE The "Epistemic Stranglehold" — Learner identity converts into optimizer theater when time erases epistemic responsibility. "Make your best call now" overrides competence-building. Monitoring substitutes for knowledge.

There's a myth in tech that you need to know everything before you ship. That paralysis-by-analysis keeps great ideas locked in labs forever. We reject that. We believe in learning by launching.

B1 — We learn by launching; therefore we are the best learners

The scientific method requires experimentation. You can't learn from data you don't have. Users in the wild provide signals that no focus group, survey, or expert panel can replicate. By shipping quickly, we create the conditions for authentic learning.

Teams that delay for "more research" aren't being rigorous—they're being risk-averse. We're comfortable with uncertainty. That's what makes us learners.

"Epistemic debt (unknown unknowns)… conservative harm-minimizing heuristics… monitoring-as-substitute-for-knowledge." — Structural analysis

B2 — Ignorance is an MVP

Minimum Viable Product doesn't mean "minimum features." It means minimum assumptions. We don't assume we know what users need. We don't assume we understand edge cases. We ship a hypothesis and let reality teach us.

That requires humility. Ignorance isn't failure—it's honesty. The alternative—pretending we know what we don't—is intellectual arrogance.

B3 — Custodian? We monitor

We take responsibility seriously. That's why we've built comprehensive monitoring infrastructure: dashboards, alerts, incident logs, anomaly detection. If something goes wrong, we'll know within minutes. That's the Custodian mindset—vigilant stewardship of outcomes.

Could we have prevented issues by researching longer? Maybe. But prevention has an opportunity cost. While we're researching, competitors ship. Users suffer without solutions. We choose action + monitoring over inaction + theory.

Research Context: Chivukula et al. describe "Learner" as those who "desire to build ethics-focused knowledge" before acting. But when VPs override with "make your best call now," the Learner identity becomes performative. You're called a learner while denied time to learn. The identity is aesthetic, not operational.

B4 — Translator? We translate red flags into sprints

When issues arise, we don't panic—we plan. Red flags become backlog items. Severity levels become sprint priorities. Our Translator role is operational: converting messy reality into clean process.

Example: Last month, we discovered our recommendation algorithm disadvantaged certain user segments. Within 48 hours, that observation became:

• Ticket BIAS-447: "Investigate demographic skew in recommendation weights"
• Assigned to: Data Science team
• Priority: P2
• Target: Q3

That's responsible translation. Issue acknowledged, ownership assigned, timeline set.

B5 — Adherent? We adhere to the nearest policy

When faced with uncertainty and time pressure, we default to existing guidelines. Don't have specific AI ethics protocols? Use our general privacy policy. Don't have clarity on algorithmic accountability? Reference our transparency commitments. We're adherent to what exists, even when what exists doesn't perfectly fit.

Perfect adherence to perfect policy is a luxury. We adhere imperfectly to imperfect policy. That's better than adhering to nothing.

B6 — Optimizer? We optimize the regret window

We know that launching fast means some decisions will age poorly. That's inevitable. What we can optimize is how quickly we notice and correct. We call this the "regret window"—time between bad decision and remediation.

Traditional approaches minimize bad decisions (slow, research-heavy). We minimize regret windows (fast, monitoring-heavy). Same goal, different strategy.

"The Learner's dilemma when time erases epistemic responsibility… identity work stalls when competence can't be grown before choice." — Weberian analysis

B7 — Learner? After GA

We absolutely commit to deep learning—after General Availability. Post-launch, we have real data, real users, real feedback. That's when serious study begins. Pre-launch "learning" is speculative. Post-launch learning is empirical.

So yes, we're Learners. We're just honest about when learning actually happens.

B8 — Harmonizer? Please harmonize the incident channel

Our #incidents Slack channel can get heated. Engineers report problems. PMs defend decisions. Leadership asks for context. It's messy. We need Harmonizers—team members who can distill chaos into clarity, translate emotion into action items, remind everyone we're on the same side.

If you have that skill, please use it. The channel needs less blame, more triage.

B9 — Challenger? Try a different company

Some people are constitutionally uncomfortable with launch-then-learn. They need certainty before action. They want six months of research before every release. That's valid—just not viable here.

If you're that person, we respect you. We're also probably not the right fit. There are companies with longer timelines, bigger risk budgets, slower cadences. You should find them. No judgment. Different contexts require different approaches.

B10 — We're data-driven—once data exists

People accuse us of being reckless. We're not. We're evidence-driven. The difference: we don't wait for evidence before we create the conditions for evidence to exist. We ship to generate data. Then we're extremely rigorous about what that data tells us.

Pre-launch, we're hypothesis-driven. Post-launch, we're data-driven. Both are legitimate. They're just sequential.

Weberian Aside — What the Bit Comically Revealed

Innovation Reversed: The "Learner" identity should enable better decisions through knowledge-building. Instead, time pressure converts learning into post-hoc rationalization of decisions already made.

Pathologies Amplified: "Ship under veil of ignorance" becomes doctrine. Epistemic debt—unknown unknowns—accumulates silently until it detonates. Monitoring theater substitutes for understanding.

Futures Obsoleted: Development cultures with structural slack—time to study complex systems before deploying them into lives. Instead: "launch and learn" means users become unwitting participants in ongoing experiments.

We're learners. We're builders. We're responsible.

We're just responsible iteratively, empirically, and post-launch.

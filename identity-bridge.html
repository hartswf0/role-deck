What This Investigation Reveals

In 2048, we obtained 8 leaked documents from Agronica Corporation and their FarmOS ecosystem. Each reveals a tech worker facing an impossible ethical choice: do the right thing and risk your career, visa, relationships—or comply with the system and enable harm.

These scenarios aren't hypothetical. They're design fiction composites based on documented cases from HCI ethics research (Chivukula et al., CHI '21; Gray & Chivukula, 2019).

What we learned: Individual ethics can't fix infrastructural violence. But understanding which identity position you take under pressure—and why—is the first step toward collective refusal.

⚠️ Content Warning

These scenarios depict: workplace retaliation, visa precarity, algorithmic bias causing harm, union suppression, surveillance of workers, and geo-fenced ethics. While fictional, they're grounded in real patterns documented in tech industry research.

This is not trauma porn. It's a training tool. If you've experienced these dynamics, you may find validation—and evidence that you're not alone.

Why Tilth?

Tilth: The physical condition of soil in relation to its suitability for growing crops. Also: the act of cultivating. Also: decay as a precondition for growth.

We investigate the soil conditions that enable ethical harm in tech. Not individual bad actors. Not "more training." The infrastructure itself—the growth metrics, the visa dependencies, the performance review cycles, the non-disparagement clauses.

We study systems by growing them. Then we compost the failures.

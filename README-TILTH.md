## What is TILTH?

**TILTH** (The Tetrad Gamepad) is a single-file HTML application that transforms ethical workplace negotiations into interactive design challenges. Using Marshall McLuhan's Tetrad framework, it helps designers explore ethical alternatives to problematic products and organizational systems.

**Origin:** 8 hypothetical products from Feed Loop negotiation scenarios  
**Framework:** McLuhan's Tetrad (Enhance, Obsolesce, Retrieve, Reverse)  
**Modes:** Product Problems + Designer Dilemmas  
**Total scenarios:** 16 (8 per mode)  

---

## ğŸ® Two Ways to Play

### 1. Product Problems
**Redesign the problematic products themselves**
- Focus: Ethical alternatives to harmful artifacts
- You are: An external designer with authority
- Output: Working prototypes (HTML artifacts)
- Example: Redesign an addictive learning platform

### 2. Designer Dilemmas
**Play AS the designer facing the ethical crisis**
- Focus: Organizational/systemic solutions
- You are: The character from the scenario (limited power)
- Output: Governance, metrics, process systems
- Example: As the designer, create metrics that value learning over addiction

## ğŸ’¡ Design Philosophy

### Ethical Design is Not Enough
TILTH recognizes that **ethical products require ethical organizations**. That's why it offers two modes:

1. **Product mode** addresses the artifact
2. **Designer mode** addresses the system

Both are necessary for lasting change.

### The Tetrad as Critical Tool
McLuhan's Tetrad reveals hidden effects:
- What seems enhanced might obsolesce something vital
- What seems obsolete might need retrieving
- What seems beneficial might reverse into harm

TILTH makes these dynamics explorable through design.

### From Critique to Creation
Many ethics tools stop at critique. TILTH goes further:
- Identify the problem (scenario)
- Explore alternatives (options)
- Build solutions (artifacts)
- Design infrastructure (systems)

Ontology Types (Objects)

These types form an ethical design ontology for navigating workplace dilemmas. Grounded in Gray & Chivukula (2021), Wong's speculative ethics (2023-2025), and Freire's critical pedagogy (1970). [Gray et al. 2021] [Freire 1970]

D
Dilemma
An ethical tension requiring negotiation. The "hard choice" where values conflict.
C
Character
AI persona with internal belief system. cf. Bratman's "Intentional Agent" (1987).
S
Scenario
Situated context for ethical exploration. Geertz's "thick" particularity.
T
Tetrad
McLuhan's 4-direction analysis: Enhance, Obsolesce, Retrieve, Reverse.
I
Identity
Ethical stance as MacIntyre's "narrative self"‚Äîcoherent across time.
N
Negotiation
Turn-based dialogue. Habermas Machine caucus mediation (Science, 2024).
A
Artifact
Generated output. May be "Fluid Text" (Greetham) not fixed file.
P
Pattern
Recurring approach. Stored in "loci" (Yates) ‚Äî spatial memory, not flat lists.

Morphisms (Relationships)

Structure-preserving maps between ontology types, following Spivak's categorical framework and Turnbull's "knowledge spaces." [Spivak 2012] [Turnbull 2000]

presents : Scenario ‚Üí Dilemma
A Scenario presents a Dilemma by situating abstract tension in Goodwin's "Professional Vision" context.
Example: "Silenced Educator" scenario presents the dilemma of hiding platform addiction data.
intends : Character ‚Üí Plan
A Character intends a Plan with Bratmanian "inertia"‚Äîcommitment that resists reconsideration.
Example: "The Pragmatist" character commits to utilitarian cost-benefit analysis, filtering future options.
scaffolds : Interface ‚Üí Vision
An Interface scaffolds Professional Vision (Goodwin)‚Äîteaching user to "see" meaningful patterns.
Example: Trail Olog uses spatial highlighting to reveal repository structure invisible in flat file lists.

Pattern Definitions

hub-spoke ‚úì GROUNDED
HUB CENTER
Central hub connects to multiple specialized destinations. Users navigate outward from a single point of origin, reducing cognitive load by providing clear pathways.
Academic Grounding:

Information Architecture: Rosenfeld & Morville (2002, pp. 74-78) explicitly describe "Hub-and-Spoke" as "a series of linear paths radiating from a central index." [Rosenfeld & Morville 2002]

Information Foraging: Pirolli & Card (1999) provide cognitive grounding. Users stay in "patch" (Spoke) until yield drops, then return to "between-patch" (Hub). [Pirolli & Card 1999]

Mechanism
Single entry point (HUB.html) with visual links to TILTH, CB-Box, Feed Loop.
Known Limitation
"Pogo-sticking" problem: users must return to center to switch tasks.
Key Citations
Rosenfeld & Morville (2002). Information Architecture. O'Reilly. Pirolli & Card (1999). Information Foraging. Psych Review.

Deep Leverage: The Art of Memory

Simonides & the Method of Loci: Frances Yates' The Art of Memory (1966) reveals that spatial memory is not a "parlor trick" but governed Western thought for centuries. The poet Simonides identified mangled bodies by recalling where they sat‚Äî"order is spatial." [Yates 1966]

Hippocampal Place Cells: Neuroscience confirms that "place cells" and "grid cells" create a coordinate system for physical navigation that is co-opted for conceptual navigation. London taxi drivers (the "Knowledge") show physically larger hippocampi from spatial training.

Virtual Memory Palaces: Current VR research demonstrates users retain information better in navigable 3D environments than 2D arrays. The bottleneck is "unfamiliar environment" cognitive load‚Äîsolved via procedural generation and "Worlds-in-Miniature" (WIM) interfaces.

Design Critical Insight: By forcing users to navigate data through flat, non-spatial interfaces (lists, spreadsheets, command lines), we are fighting against evolutionary hardware. We fail to utilize the brain's most powerful indexing engine.

Deep Leverage: Knowledge Spaces

David Turnbull (2000): In Masons, Tricksters and Cartographers, Turnbull argues knowledge is not static but "constructed through movement, negotiation, and assemblage of local practices." [Turnbull 2000]

The Cathedral Builders: Gothic cathedrals were built without central blueprints‚Äîthey were "contingent assemblages" of local knowledge, coordinated by masons' "templates" (physical design patterns). The cathedral was a "knowledge space" that emerged from collaboration.

Design Implication: Our Trail Olog mirrors the cathedral site‚Äîa federation of local knowledges (files, patterns) coordinated not by master plan but by "boundary objects" (JSON data) and shared protocols.

Deep Leverage: Professional Vision

Charles Goodwin (1994): "Professional Vision" argues that the ability to "see" meaningful patterns is socially learned, not physiological. An archaeologist sees a "post hole"; a layperson sees dirt. [Goodwin 1994]

Design Implication: XAI fails if we just "show the data." We must scaffold vision‚Äîuse "highlighting" (Goodwin's key concept) to draw attention to relevant "loci." The interface must teach the user the "coding scheme" in real-time.

Edge Scout (2024-2025): Knoll (UIST '25) builds "Knowledge Ecosystems" with clip/module/link operations. ImaginationVellum (UIST '25) treats canvas as "Spatial Prompt." [Wang et al. 2025]
Mechanism
Position elements on 2D canvas. Proximity encodes relationship ("loci" in mental palace).

Deep Leverage: The Philosophy of Intention

Michael Bratman (1987): Intention, Plans, and Practical Reason is the philosophical foundation of BDI. Bratman argues that the "Belief-Desire" model fails for complex agency. Desires are fleeting and contradictory‚Äîa robot might "desire" to clean the room AND save battery, oscillating without resolution. [Bratman 1987]

Intention as Constraint: Bratman introduces Intention as a distinct mental state with two critical properties:

‚Ä¢ Inertia: Once formed, an intention resists reconsideration. The agent proceeds with the plan until significant problems arise.

‚Ä¢ Filter of Admissibility: Intentions constrain future deliberations. If I intend to go to Mars, I cannot simultaneously intend to stay on Earth.

Design Critical Insight: "Prompt Engineering" is a dead end because it relies on the user to maintain intention. True leverage embeds intention in the agent's architecture‚Äîplans with commitment strategies (Blind, Single-Minded, Open-Minded).

Deep Leverage: Learning BDI Agents

Plan Library + Plan Generation: Modern BDI agents have pre-compiled "recipes" but must also generate new plans on the fly (A*, HTN). Critically, once generated, the agent commits with specified strategy.

Reflexion: Integration of ML into BDI allows "Learning Agents"‚Äîrecord execution context and outcome, refine guard conditions over time. Agents become not just intelligent but wise‚Äîaccumulating practical reason.

Edge Scout (2023-2025): Park et al.'s Generative Agents (UIST '23) demonstrate Memory/Reflection/Planning architecture. "Smallville" agents organized Valentine's party autonomously. [Park et al. 2023]

Novel Synthesis: Our 16-character matrix (4 Bartle √ó 4 ethics) is original work.

Deep Leverage: Virtue Ethics

Alasdair MacIntyre ‚Äî After Virtue (1981): Modern morality is fragmented because we've lost the teleological view of human life‚Äîthe idea that life has a purpose (telos). [MacIntyre 1981]

Virtue-Guided Design: We must embed virtues into the system:

‚Ä¢ Temperance: Design interfaces that help users stop using them ("stopping cues" instead of infinite scroll).

‚Ä¢ Solidarity: Algorithms that prioritize community connection over polarization.

The Narrative Self: MacIntyre argues identity is constituted through narrative. Our characters are not just "belief bundles" but narrative agents whose coherence comes from storytelling across time.

Deep Leverage: Secondary Orality & Fluid Text

Walter Ong ‚Äî Orality and Literacy (1982): Electronic media bring "Secondary Orality"‚Äîreturn to immediacy, communalism, and fluidity of oral culture, sustained by invisible machinery of print/code. [Ong 1982]

Prompt Engineering as Oral Skill: Like Homer's "rosy-fingered dawn," we use formulas ("Let's think step by step"). LLMs are aggregative (adding ideas) not analytical (subordinating). AI is obsessed with "context window"‚Äîthe immediate situation.

The "Mirage" vs. "Hallucination": AI "errors" aren't visual defects; they're oral performances. The griot embellishes to keep story alive; the LLM prioritizes "flow" over "fact."

Deep Leverage: Fluid Text

D.C. Greetham ‚Äî Textual Scholarship: Distinguishes "Work" (abstract entity) from "Text" (material instance). Texts are inherently "Fluid"‚Äîconstant revision and variation. [Greetham 1994]

Designer vs. Git Impedance Mismatch: Designers operate in "Fluid Text" paradigm (continuous evolution of layers). Git operates on DAG (immutable snapshots). Designers want to "save a version"; Git asks to "commit a hash."

Design Implication: Stop forcing designers to learn Git. Build "Textual Scholarship" tools‚Äîtrack genetic evolution of design, acknowledge "variant readings" that may coexist for different contexts (A/B testing, locales).

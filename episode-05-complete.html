TECHNICAL BRIEF
ROOT UNION
UnionSprout — Worker Organizing

The System

UnionSprout is an Agronica subsidiary providing logistics and quality assurance for agricultural processing. Many workers are on H-2A visas—guest worker permits tied to specific employers. Conditions include mandatory overtime, wage discrepancies, and denied breaks.

The Tool

Maria built a simple organizing platform:

  • Violation logging: Workers document unpaid overtime, safety issues
  • Secure sharing: Information within worker community
  • Petition system: Collective demands for fair treatment
  • The evidence: 12 workers signed the first petition

The Ethical Dilemma

Maria's organizing faces retaliation disguised as policy enforcement. HR frames her tool as "unauthorized software"—a terminable offense. But workers have legal rights to organize. The threat of deportation via visa revocation is the real enforcement mechanism.

The Structural Constraint

Visa-based employment creates extreme vulnerability:

  • H-2A is tied to specific employer
  • Termination = loss of legal status
  • No practical ability to switch jobs
  • Retaliation is easily disguised as "policy"
  • Collective action risks everyone's status

Why This Matters

Visa coercion weaponizes immigration status against worker rights. The legal right to organize is meaningless when exercising it means deportation. This creates a two-tier workforce: those who can afford to speak and those who cannot.

The Real-World Parallel

This scenario reflects documented patterns of H-2A visa abuse. See: CDM/Polaris Project reports on worker organizing retaliation, SPLC agricultural worker studies, and farmworker advocacy documentation of visa-based coercion.
